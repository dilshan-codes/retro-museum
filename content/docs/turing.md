# Turing Machine

## What Is It?
The Turing Machine is not a physical machine — it is a mathematical idea 
invented by Alan Turing in 1936. It describes the simplest possible computer 
that can solve any problem that is computable.

## Who Was Alan Turing?
Alan Turing was a British mathematician born in 1912. He is considered the 
father of computer science and artificial intelligence. During World War II 
he led the team that broke the Nazi Enigma code, an achievement that is 
believed to have shortened the war by two years.

## How Does It Work?
The machine has three parts:
- An infinite tape divided into cells, each holding a symbol
- A read/write head that moves along the tape one cell at a time
- A set of rules that tell the head what to do based on what it reads

At each step the machine reads the current cell, decides what to write 
and which direction to move, then changes its internal state. That is all 
it does — yet this simple process can compute anything a modern computer can.

## Why Does It Matter?
Before Turing, nobody had a precise definition of what computation meant. 
Turing gave the world a clear answer. Every computer ever built — from the 
Manchester Baby to your smartphone — is essentially a Turing Machine with 
faster hardware.
