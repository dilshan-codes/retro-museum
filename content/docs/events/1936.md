# 1936 — The Turing Machine

## The Year Everything Started
In 1936, a 24-year-old British mathematician named Alan Turing published a 
paper called "On Computable Numbers." In it he described a theoretical 
machine that could solve any mathematical problem that is computable. Nobody 
had ever precisely defined what computation meant before this moment.

## The Machine
Turing imagined an infinitely long tape divided into cells. A read/write 
head moves along the tape, reading symbols and writing new ones according 
to a set of rules. The machine has internal states that change as it works. 
That is all — yet this simple description defines the limits of what any 
computer can ever do.

## Why 1936 Matters
Every computer ever built is a physical implementation of Turing's idea. 
When you run an app on your phone, play a game, or send a message, you are 
using a machine that Turing described mathematically in 1936 — eleven years 
before the first real computer was even built.

## Alan Turing
Turing went on to lead the codebreaking effort at Bletchley Park during 
World War II, cracking the Nazi Enigma cipher and helping end the war early. 
He later laid the foundations for artificial intelligence. In 2021 his face 
was placed on the British 50 pound note — a recognition of arguably the most 
important intellectual contribution of the 20th century.
