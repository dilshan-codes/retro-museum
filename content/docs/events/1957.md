# 1957 — The First Digital Image

## A Photo That Changed Everything
In 1957, Russell Kirsch at the US National Bureau of Standards scanned a 
small photograph of his three month old son Walden into a computer. The 
image measured 176 x 176 pixels. It was the first digital image ever created 
and it laid the foundation for every photograph, video, and screen you have 
ever seen.

## How It Was Done
Kirsch mounted the photo on a rotating drum scanner. As the drum spun, a 
sensor measured the brightness at each point and converted it into a number. 
Those numbers were fed into a computer — the first time a visual image had 
ever been represented as digital data.

## What Is A Pixel?
The word pixel comes from picture element. Each pixel is a single point of 
color or brightness in a digital image. Kirsch's first image had 176 x 176 
equals 30,976 pixels total. The photo on your phone today has tens of 
millions. The idea is exactly the same — just scaled up enormously.

## The Legacy
Kirsch's work directly led to satellite imagery, CT scans, MRI machines, 
digital cameras, computer displays, and every screen you look at today. 
Every pixel on the screen you are reading this on exists because Russell 
Kirsch tried to get a picture of his baby son into a computer in 1957.
