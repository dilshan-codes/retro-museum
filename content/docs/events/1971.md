# 1971 — The Microprocessor

## The Entire CPU On One Chip
On November 15, 1971, Intel released the 4004 — the world's first commercial 
microprocessor. For the first time in history an entire central processing 
unit fit on a single silicon chip. It had 2,300 transistors and was originally 
designed for a calculator made by a Japanese company called Busicom.

## What Is A Microprocessor?
Before the 4004, a computer's CPU was built from many separate chips and 
components spread across large circuit boards. A microprocessor puts all of 
that — the arithmetic unit, the control unit, the registers — onto one tiny 
chip. This made computers small enough and cheap enough to put into products.

## Why It Matters
The Intel 4004 started a chain reaction. Within four years Intel released 
the 8080, which inspired the Altair 8800 — the first personal computer kit. 
Bill Gates and Paul Allen wrote software for it and founded Microsoft. 
The microprocessor turned computing from a government and university tool 
into something that would eventually sit in every pocket on earth.
