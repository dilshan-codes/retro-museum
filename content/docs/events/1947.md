# 1947 — The Transistor

## The Invention That Changed Everything
On December 23, 1947, three scientists at Bell Labs in New Jersey — William 
Shockley, John Bardeen, and Walter Brattain — demonstrated the first working 
transistor. This single invention is arguably the most important in human 
history. It won them the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1956.

## What Is A Transistor?
A transistor is a tiny electronic switch. It controls the flow of electricity 
through a circuit — turning it on or off. Before transistors, computers used 
vacuum tubes to do this job. Vacuum tubes were the size of a light bulb, 
burned hot, failed constantly, and consumed enormous power.

## Vacuum Tube vs Transistor
| | Vacuum Tube | Transistor |
|---|---|---|
| Size | Light bulb | Fingernail |
| Power | Very high | Very low |
| Heat | Extreme | Minimal |
| Reliability | Failed often | Very reliable |
| Cost | Expensive | Cheap to make |

## The Ripple Effect
Within ten years transistors replaced vacuum tubes in computers entirely. 
Within twenty years engineers learned to print thousands of transistors onto 
a single silicon chip — the integrated circuit. Today a single chip the size 
of a fingernail contains over 25 billion transistors. Every single one is a 
direct descendant of what Shockley, Bardeen and Brattain built in 1947.
